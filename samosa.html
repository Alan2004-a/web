SAMOSA

INGREDIENTS:

FOR THE FILLING

  • 2 tablespoons oil

  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds (optional)

  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced

  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)

  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder

  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin

  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander

  • 1 teaspoon garam masala

  • Salt, to taste

  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)

  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

FOR THE DOUGH

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour

  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee

  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds (optional)

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • Water, as needed

PREPARATION

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large pan, heat the oil over medium heat

  2. Sauté Spices: Add the cumin seeds (and mustard seeds, if using) and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Add Aromatics: Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until the onion is soft and golden.

  4. Cook Potatoes: Add the diced potatoes and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Add Spices: Stir in the turmeric, cumin, coriander, and salt. Cook for another 2 minutes.

  6. Add Peas: Add the peas and cook until the potatoes are tender and the peas are heated through. If the mixture seems dry, add a splash of water.

  7. Finish Filling: Stir in the garam masala and lemon juice (if using). Add chopped cilantro if desired. Allow the filling to cool.

  8. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, caraway seeds (if using), and salt. Add the oil or ghee and mix with your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  9. Form Dough: Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead until you have a smooth, pliable dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  10. Divide Dough: Divide the dough into golf-ball-sized portions.

  11. Roll Out Dough: On a floured surface, roll each portion into a thin oval or circle (about 6 inches in diameter). Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.

  12. Shape Samosas: Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge with a little water. Fill the cone with the potato filling, then seal the open edge by pinching the sides together to form a triangle shape. Ensure there are no gaps.

  13. Seal Edges: You can crimp the edges for a decorative touch

  14. Heat Oil: Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C).

  15. Fry Samosas: Fry the samosas in batches, turning them occasionally, until golden brown and crispy (about 4-5 minutes per batch). Drain on paper towels.
